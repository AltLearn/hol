# Happy Onlife

[![HappyOnlife](https://github.com/happyonlife/hol/blob/master/logo_hol.png)](https://ec.europa.eu/jrc/en/scientific-tool/happy-onlife-game-raise-awareness-internet-risks-and-opportunities?search)

An open-source video game for children (8-12) and adults to play and learn how to have a healthy digital life. Happy Onlife is an edutainment tool developed by the Directorate-General Joint Research Centre of the European Commission.

For more information on purpose and research visit the [Happy Onlife website](https://web.jrc.ec.europa.eu/happyonlife/) and the [JRC Science Hub of the European Commission](http://europa.eu/!pD47hy).

The software is licensed under the [EUPL, version 1.1](http://joinup.ec.europa.eu/software/page/eupl) or - as soon they will be approved by the European Commission - subsequent version of the EUPL. 

## Overview 
Happy Onlife is aimed at raising awareness on the risks and opportunities of Internet by promoting online best practices in

  - online safety
  - cyber security
  - cyberbullying
  - privacy
  - netiquette
  - digital identity management 

Current version is translated in EN, ES, FR, IT, NL.

JRC technical report describing the process behind the development of Happy Onlife is available [here](http://europa.eu/!FD84FK).

## How to play
Happy Onlife is a moderated quiz game to be played in teams of children and adults.

Quiz questions on the use of internet, social networks and online-games are designed to prompt discussion 
and allow the moderator to drive the players towards a responsible and balanced way of using digital media.

A ready-to-play version is available for download in the mobile stores of the following platforms:

- [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=ec.europa.publications.happyonlife&hl=en)
- [iTunes](https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/happy-onlife/id1017319944?mt=8)
- [Windows Phone](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/store/p/happy-onlife/9nblggh1nzhm)

A web version is also available [online](http://web.jrc.ec.europa.eu/happyonlife).

## Contribute
The software was developed using Unity3D (version Unity 5.5.1f1).

You are welcome to contribute with new features and extensions to help make learning online safety a better playful experience.

You can contact our team at the following email address: jrc-happyonlife (AT) ec.europa.eu
